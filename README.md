# neobank-notes

Personal research notes comparing neobanks and digital banking apps. Updated as I test them. Not financial advice.

## Apps covered

| App | Type | FDIC insured | Monthly fee |
|-----|------|-------------|-------------|
| Chime | Neobank | Yes (via Bancorp/Stride) | $0 |
| Cash App | Payment + investing | Yes (via Sutton Bank) | $0 |
| Venmo | P2P payments | No (Venmo balance is not insured) | $0 |
| SoFi | Full digital bank | Yes | $0 |
| Revolut | Multi-currency app | Partial (varies by country) | $0 to $16 |

## Key things I learned

- FDIC insurance applies to the partner bank, not the neobank itself. Your money is still protected, but it matters to understand who actually holds it.
- - Fee-free overdraft is a real differentiator. Chime SpotMe is one of the few that does this well without hidden conditions.
  - - APY rates change often. Always verify the current rate directly on the bank's website before opening an account.
    - - Venmo balances held in the app are not FDIC insured unless moved to the Venmo Savings account. Good to know before leaving large amounts there.
      - - Cash App investing is useful for small amounts but is not a substitute for a real brokerage account.
